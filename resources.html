Resources

Tips, guides, and answers to help you stay ahead of vehicle maintenance.

Maintenance Tips

Check Your Oil Regularly

Engine oil should be checked monthly and changed every 5,000–7,500 miles depending on your vehicle and oil type. Never ignore a low oil warning.

Monitor Tire Pressure

Proper inflation improves fuel economy and safety. Check tire pressure at least monthly — especially when temperatures change significantly.

Replace Wiper Blades

Wiper blades should be replaced every 6–12 months or when you notice streaking or skipping. Don't wait until a rainstorm to find out they've worn out.

Listen to Your Brakes

Squealing or grinding brakes are a sign that pads or rotors need attention. Have them inspected immediately — brake health is a safety issue.

Keep Fluids Topped Off

Coolant, brake fluid, power steering fluid, and windshield washer fluid all need to be maintained. Low fluid levels can cause significant damage.

Don't Ignore Warning Lights

Your dashboard warning lights exist for a reason. A check engine light or oil pressure warning should be addressed quickly — not driven on for weeks.

Suggested Maintenance Schedule

Service Frequency Notes
Oil & Filter Change Every 5,000–7,500 miles Varies by oil type and vehicle
Tire Rotation Every 5,000–7,500 miles Extend tire life and ensure even wear
Brake Inspection Every 12,000 miles Or whenever you notice changes in braking
Air Filter Replacement Every 15,000–30,000 miles Check annually; sooner in dusty conditions
Tire Alignment Every 12 months Or after hitting a major pothole or curb
Coolant Flush Every 30,000 miles Prevents overheating and corrosion
Transmission Fluid Every 30,000–60,000 miles Check owner's manual for your vehicle
Spark Plugs Every 30,000–100,000 miles Depends on plug type (copper vs iridium)
Timing Belt Every 60,000–100,000 miles Critical — failure can destroy an engine

Common Questions

How do I know if my brakes need replacing?

Listen for squealing, grinding, or a pulsing feeling when you brake. If your pedal feels soft or you need extra distance to stop, come see us right away.

What does the check engine light mean?

It can signal anything from a loose gas cap to a misfiring engine. We'll plug in a diagnostic scanner and tell you exactly what's triggering it.

How often should I really change my oil?

The old "every 3,000 miles" rule is outdated for most modern vehicles. Most synthetic oils last 5,000–7,500 miles. Check your owner's manual or ask us.

Is it safe to drive with a tire pressure warning?

A low tire is a safety risk. Pull over safely, check your pressure, and add air. If the light stays on after inflating, bring it in — you may have a slow leak.
